What this test is actually measuring

JBTI is built on four established frameworks in career psychology.

This isn't a Buzzfeed quiz with a piña colada at the end. (Okay, there's a piña colada at the end.) The 4-axis structure synthesizes work from four research traditions — and the Delulu Score is a measurable gap, not a vibe.

Methodologically reviewed by 500+ Heads of HR · §4.7(a) · No piña coladas were harmed
01
Myers-Briggs 16-type model

The same 4-axis binary structure that gave you INTJ or ENFP — adapted with four career-relevant axes: Grinder/Lounger, Money/Purpose, Social/Solo, Insider/Outsider. 16 archetypes, mapped to real career trajectories.

02
Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions

Geert Hofstede's IBM research across 50+ countries identified that work-orientation traits cluster along measurable axes. JBTI's Insider/Outsider axis maps to his findings on power-distance and uncertainty-avoidance.

03
The GLOBE Project

The Global Leadership and Organizational Behavior Effectiveness study analyzed 17,000 managers across 62 cultures. It validated that career-orientation traits stay stable across contexts. JBTI's Grinder/Lounger axis builds on GLOBE's performance-orientation work.

04
500,000 graduate outcomes

Our algorithm is trained on outcome data from recent graduates — mapping archetypes to actual career destinations. The Delulu Score is the measurable gap between your archetype and your stated dream job, not vibes.

05 · The piece nobody else has
A live network of Heads of HR at the companies you actually want to work at.
